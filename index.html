TF-SUM: Training-free Video Summarization via Language-based Hierarchical Scoring

Paper Code arXiv

Abstract

Video summarization aims to condense long and fragmented videos into concise representations that capture the most salient content. However, existing training-based methods often suffer from poor generalization across diverse video domains and depend heavily on manually annotated datasets,which limits their scalability and adaptability. To address these challenges, we propose TF-SUM, a novel Training-Free video SUMmarization framework that leverages the complementary strengths of pre-trained Large Vision-Language Models (LVLMs) and Large Language Models (LLMs). Unlike conventional approaches, TF-Sum does not require any model training or supervision. It first uses a VLM-based captioning model to generate textual descriptions for each frame and filters noisy outputs via cross-modal similarity-based cleaning. Then, an LLM is employed to conduct hierarchical importance scoring, generating both fine-grained (frame-level) and coarse-grained (segment-level) importance estimates based purely on textual summaries. Finally, visual similarity is utilized to aggregate semantically redundant frames and refine the importance distribution. Extensive experiments on two public benchmarks, TVSum and SumMe, demonstrate that TF-SUM achieves competitive or superior performance compared to state-of-the-art unsupervised methods, without any training or annotations.
